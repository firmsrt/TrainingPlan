📖 What the words mean

Easy run
Run slowly — slow enough to hold a conversation. Most of your running is this. If your heart rate climbs over ~150, slow down or walk a bit.
Long run
The week's longest run, done at the same easy pace. Walk breaks are totally fine — finishing the distance matters more than speed.
Strides (e.g. "4–6 × 20s strides")
At the end of an easy run, do 4 to 6 short bursts of about 20 seconds each: smoothly speed up to a quick-but-relaxed pace (not a sprint), then slow right down and walk ~40 seconds until your breathing settles. Repeat. They wake up your legs and improve running form. On a treadmill: bump to ~9.5 km/h for 20 seconds, then back to a walk.
Tempo run
An easy warm-up, then a stretch at a "comfortably-hard" pace — working, but controlled (you can only say a few words at a time), then an easy cool-down. This teaches your body to hold a faster pace.
Intervals
After a warm-up, run several short faster pieces (e.g. 3 minutes at goal race pace) with an easy jog between each to recover, then cool down. Sharpens your speed.
1% incline
On the treadmill, set the slope to 1%. It makes indoor running feel a little more like running outside.
